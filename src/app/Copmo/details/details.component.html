About Torna Fort

Torna Fort, located near Pune in Maharashtra, is one of the most significant historical and trekking destinations in the region. Also known as Prachandagad, it stands as the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1643, marking the beginning of the Maratha Empire. At an elevation of around 4,603 feet, Torna Fort offers a challenging yet thrilling trek with stunning views of the Sahyadri mountain range. The trek route is adorned with lush greenery during the monsoon season and becomes an adventure-filled hike for enthusiasts. The fort also has key landmarks such as the Zunjar Machi and Budhla Machi, which offer panoramic views of the surroundings. Torna's rich history combined with its natural beauty makes it a popular destination for trekkers and history buffs alike.

Explore Torna Points
  • Zunjar Machi: A narrow ridge with stunning views, perfect for adventurous trekkers.
  • Budhla Machi: Unique rock formations offering scenic vistas of the surrounding valleys.
  • Tornaji Kada: A cliff providing panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountains, ideal for photography.
  • Kothi Darwaja: The main entrance showcasing ancient Maratha architecture and history.
  • Menghai Devi Temple: A sacred temple at the top, offering rest and spiritual peace for trekkers.
